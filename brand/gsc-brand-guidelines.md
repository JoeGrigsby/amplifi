# The Gruene Songwriters Collective — Brand Guidelines

> *Gruene, Texas · Est. Summer 2025*
> *★ Americana · Country · Folk ★*

---

## Brand Purpose

A local community of singer-songwriters creating together, not alone — anchored to a specific place and a shared respect for the craft. Where the song is the point of everything.

---

## Positioning

The Gruene Songwriters Collective is the proof-of-concept and the soul of AmpliFi — the first collective in the network, built in the heartland of Americana music. It holds a dual identity:

1. **A real artist community** with real shows, real songs, and real relationships built around a shared creative practice
2. **A replicable model** that other regional collectives can follow as AmpliFi scales

The GSC is where AmpliFi's mission becomes human. If AmpliFi is the architecture, the GSC is the people who live in it.

### Origin
Formed in the summer of 2025 when a group of aspiring and seasoned writers gathered for a three-day workshop in Gruene, Texas, led by acclaimed songwriters Jason Eady and Adam Hood. The place shaped the community as much as the workshop did. Gruene — with its legendary dance hall, cypress-shaded river bends, and unhurried pace — has a way of pulling the truth out of a song.

## Tone of Voice

### Four Principles

**1. Rooted**
Deeply specific. Gruene, Texas. The Guadalupe River. Americana. The Texas Hill Country. The San Marcos corridor. Place matters here — it's not backdrop, it's identity. When in doubt, name the place, not the feeling.

**2. Literary**
Elegant, unhurried prose. The GSC writes the way a great lyric lands: with economy and weight. A sentence should do one thing well. Avoid decoration. Earn every adjective.

**3. Honest**
Never oversells. The truth of the work is the sell. If a show was small and intimate, say so — that's a virtue, not an apology. The GSC doesn't chase hype; it documents something real.

**4. Communal**
"We" before "I." The collective is the thing, not any single artist. Individual artist voices are celebrated, but the GSC's own communications always foreground the group.

## Voice: Sounds Like / Doesn't Sound Like

### ✓ Sounds like the GSC
- *"Where songs are built one story at a time."*
- *"You can't fake it there."*
- *"A place where friendships take root, ideas sharpen, and each writer finds their voice."*
- *"We don't all sound the same. And that's exactly the point."*
- *"From the hills of Gruene to the shores of Canyon Lake."*
- *"Built on shared accountability, creative momentum, and real community."*

### ✗ Doesn't sound like the GSC
- "Curating amazing talent"
- "Connecting passionate creators"
- "Our journey as a community"
- "Bringing music to the next level"
- "An incredible lineup of artists"
- "Excited to announce"

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Midnight | `#1a1208` | Primary background |
| Gold | `#c8a45a` | Primary accent, star marks, highlights |
| Parchment | `#f2ead8` | Light backgrounds, body text on dark |
| Barn Red | `#8b2020` | Secondary accent, CTAs, section markers |
| Warm Brown | `#3d2b1a` | Body text on light backgrounds |

### Typography
- **Display / Headlines:** Playfair Display (serif) — weight and warmth
- **Labels / Navigation:** Oswald (condensed, uppercase, tracked) — directional, utilitarian
- **Body:** Crimson Pro — literary, readable, humanist

### Photography
- Monochrome (black and white) strongly preferred
- Candid over posed — live performances, rehearsals, outdoor gatherings
- Human scale — the face, the hands on the guitar, the circle of writers around a table
- Texas landscape used sparingly as context, not as aesthetic filler

### Mood Reference
Old Texas dance hall. Firelight. Weathered wood. Screen door in summer. Cypress trees at dusk. The kind of place where the conversation goes quiet when someone starts to play.

### Texture
Letterpress, screenprint, hand-drawn elements. Analog warmth. Event posters should feel like they could be pinned to a corkboard at the feed store.

| Fan Membership | Belonging language — fans are part of the community, not just an audience |

## The Sentence That Defines This Brand

> *"You can't fake it there."*

This line, from the GSC's own story copy, is the north star for voice. It captures the place, the honesty, the standard, and the ethos in five words. When writing for the GSC, ask: does this feel like something you can fake? If yes, rewrite it.

---

## Relationship to AmpliFi

AmpliFi is the **mission and the infrastructure**.
The GSC is the **soul and the proof**.

The GSC carries the warmth, the place, the people, and the specific human story that makes AmpliFi's broader mission believable. In presentations, grant applications, and sponsor conversations, the GSC is the example. In its own communications, it doesn't need to carry that weight — it just needs to be itself.
