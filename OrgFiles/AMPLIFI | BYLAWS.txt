# AMPLIFI | BYLAWS

**Adopted:** May 3, 2026  
**Entity:** AmpliFi, an Illinois Not-for-Profit Corporation

## ARTICLE I — NAME AND PURPOSE

**Name:**  
The name of this corporation is **AmpliFi**.

**Purpose:**  
AmpliFi is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes, specifically to support independent singer-songwriters through:

- Community infrastructure  
- Touring networks  
- Studio access  
- Educational resources  

All in service of sustaining the creative and economic life of working artists.

---

## ARTICLE II — BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 2.1 — Composition

- Minimum: **3 directors**  
- Maximum: **9 directors**

**Initial Board:**
- Joseph C. Grigsby — Western Springs, IL  
- Nathan Young — Eudora, KS  
- Timothy Eavenson — Western Springs, IL  

---

### Section 2.2 — Terms

- Term length: **2 years**
- Eligible for re-election: **Yes (no limit)**

---

### Section 2.3 — Meeting Frequency

- Minimum: **4 meetings per year (quarterly)**
- Format:
  - In person
  - Phone
  - Video conference
- Special meetings:
  - May be called by:
    - President, or
    - Any two directors
  - Notice: **72 hours**

---

### Section 2.4 — Quorum

- Quorum: **Majority of directors**
- Voting: **Majority vote of those present with quorum**

---

### Section 2.5 — Annual Meeting

- Held each **January**
- Purpose:
  - Review financials
  - Elect officers
  - Set organizational priorities

---

## ARTICLE IV — CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

### Section 4.1 — Purpose

To protect AmpliFi’s interests in transactions involving potential conflicts.

---

### Section 4.2 — Disclosure

- Any financial interest must be disclosed prior to Board vote

---

### Section 4.3 — Recusal

- Interested individual must:
  - Leave discussion and voting
- Remaining Board determines:
  - Whether conflict exists
  - Whether transaction is acceptable

---

### Section 4.4 — Documentation

- All disclosures recorded in meeting minutes  
- Annual signed acknowledgment required  

---

### Section 4.5 — No Loans

- No loans permitted to:
  - Directors  
  - Officers  
  - Employees  

---

## ARTICLE V — FINANCIAL MATTERS

### Section 5.1 — Fiscal Year

- January 1 – December 31  

---

### Section 5.2 — Bank Accounts

- Funds held in federally insured institution  
- Disbursements over $1,000:
  - Require **two officer signatures**

---

### Section 5.3 — No Private Inurement

- No earnings may benefit:
  - Directors  
  - Officers  
  - Private individuals  

- Exception:
  - Reasonable compensation for services  

---

## ARTICLE VI — DISSOLUTION

### Section 6.1 — Procedure

- Requires:
  - **2/3 vote of full Board**
  - **14 days written notice**

---

### Section 6.2 — Distribution of Assets

- Assets distributed to:
  - Qualified 501(c)(3) organizations  
- No distribution to individuals  

**Assumed successor organization:**
- The Gruene Songwriters Collective  
- Or comparable music-focused nonprofit  

---

## ARTICLE VII — AMENDMENTS

- Requires:
  - **2/3 Board vote**
  - Written notice **7 days prior**

---

## ARTICLE VIII — PARLIAMENTARY AUTHORITY

- Governed by **Robert’s Rules of Order** when not otherwise specified
