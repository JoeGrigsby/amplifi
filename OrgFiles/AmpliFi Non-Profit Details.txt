## Articles of Incorporation

The **Articles of Incorporation** is the legal document used to form a corporation.

### Requirements

- Submit **two copies** (one original)
- Use **Form NFP 102.10**
- Include:
  - Corporate name
  - Registered agent and office
  - Directors
  - Purpose
  - Incorporators

---

## Corporate Name Rules

- Must be **distinct** from existing entities
- May require **“NFP” designation**
- Cannot include political party names without approval

### Name Reservation

- Reserve for 90 days
- Fee: **$25**

---

## Registered Agent & Office

### Requirements

- Must be:
  - Illinois resident **OR**
  - Authorized business entity
- Must have a **physical Illinois address (no P.O. boxes)**

Failure to maintain this can result in dissolution.

---

## Purpose of Corporation

Must be **specific and narrow**

### Acceptable Purpose Categories

- Charitable
- Educational
- Religious
- Scientific
- Civic
- Literary
- Agricultural
- Research
- Social
- Professional/trade associations
- Community services
- Cooperative services

> Avoid vague or business-oriented language.

---

## Directors & Incorporators

- Minimum **3 directors**
- Incorporators:
  - At least one
  - Must be 18+ or a legal entity

---

## Filing the Articles

### Requirements

- Completed forms (2 copies)
- Filing fee: **$50**

### Payment Methods

- Credit card
- Certified check
- Cashier’s check
- Money order
- Attorney/CPA check

---

## After Incorporation

### Federal Tax

- Apply via IRS (Form 1023 or 1024)
- Incorporation required before applying

### Illinois Tax

- Federal exemption = Illinois exemption
- Otherwise must file state taxes

### Sales Tax

- Possible exemption for:
  - Charitable
  - Religious
  - Educational organizations

### Attorney General Registration

- Required for some charitable organizations

---

## Reporting Requirements

### Annual Reports

- Filed with Secretary of State
- Due **before anniversary month**
- Missing filings may result in dissolution

### Other Reporting

- IRS filings
- Illinois Department of Revenue filings
- Attorney General filings
